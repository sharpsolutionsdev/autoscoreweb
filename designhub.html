The "Netflix-Style" Subscription Flow

A highly optimized, low-friction funnel. Let them play for free immediately, lock them out exactly when they are hooked, and require a card to continue via recurring billing.

1

10-Minute Free Play

User downloads the app and opens it. No account required yet. They get exactly 10 minutes of unrestricted voice-scoring gameplay to experience the "Aha!" moment.

2

The Paywall Drops

At exactly 10 minutes, the screen locks. A popup appears: "Preview Ended. Start your 7-Day Free Trial to continue playing." They are sent to Stripe Checkout.

3

Recurring Billing

User enters card info. Stripe grants 7 free days. On Day 7, Stripe automatically charges £6.99, and continues monthly until they cancel (just like Spotify or Netflix). If they don't cancel, you get paid automatically.

Scaling & Monetization

How to increase your Lifetime Value (LTV) and generate more revenue beyond the basic £6.99/mo subscription.

B2B "Pub & Club" Licenses

The Play: Sell a "Commercial Hardware Kit" (a cheap £100 Android tablet pre-loaded with DartVoice + a Bluetooth directional mic) to local pubs.

Pricing: £30/month per dartboard. Pubs pay this eagerly because automatic scoring keeps players drinking instead of doing math.

Celebrity Voice Packs (DLC)

The Play: Instead of standard text-to-speech, partner with actual darts callers (like Russ Bray) or players.

Pricing: Sell the "Russ Bray Voice Pack" as a one-time £4.99 In-App Purchase. Pure profit impulse buy for existing subscribers.

The "Watch an Ad to Play" Model

The Play: Some users will never put a credit card in. Don't lose them. After their 10 mins expire, give them an option: "Watch a 30-second video ad to unlock 10 more minutes."

Revenue: You get paid by Google AdMob, and they eventually get annoyed by the ads and upgrade to the £6.99/mo plan anyway.

Paid Online Tournaments

The Play: Host a monthly "DartVoice Global Open" directly in the app.

Pricing: £2.99 entry fee. If 500 people enter, that's £1,500. Give £500 to the winner, and you keep £1,000 profit for just setting up a Supabase leaderboard.
