A fairer ladder

HOW RANKED WORKS

Ranked Series uses an Elo-style MMR system. Your rank reflects who you actually beat — not just how many darts you throw or how high your average is. Win against stronger opponents, climb fast. Lose to weaker ones, drop fast. Skill, not volume.

Wins matter more than averages

A player who averages 75 but consistently beats higher-ranked opponents will outrank a 95-average player who only wins easy matches. MMR rewards who you beat, not your raw stats.

PDC-style order, dynamic placement

Like the PDC Order of Merit, your rank is relative to the field. Unlike it, MMR moves every match — you don't have to wait two years for your prize money to expire. Form is felt in days, not seasons.

10 placement matches

Everyone starts at 1200 MMR. Your first 10 matches are placement — your tier is hidden while we figure out where you actually slot in. After that, every match counts toward your global leaderboard position.

The maths, in plain English

Beating someone stronger

If your MMR is 1200 and you beat someone at 1500, you'll gain +25 to +30 MMR — the system thought you'd lose, so it adjusts heavily.

Beating someone weaker

Beating a player 300 below you only nets +5 to +10 — you were expected to win.

Losing to a stronger player

You'll lose just 5–10 MMR. The system already had them ahead — no need to punish you for the upset that didn't happen.

Losing to a weaker player

That'll cost you 25–30 MMR. Big upsets shake the ladder fast — that's the whole point.

Plus a small leg-margin bonus: a 5–0 sweep gives a touch more than a 3–2 grind.
