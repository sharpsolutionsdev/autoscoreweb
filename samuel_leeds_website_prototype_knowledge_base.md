# Samuel Leeds Website Prototype Knowledge Base

## 1. Context

This document is designed to help an AI or developer fully understand the situation, goals, and direction for creating a high-quality prototype website.

Goal: Build a prototype that improves upon the current Samuel Leeds website by increasing perceived premium quality, improving user experience, and maximising conversion and monetisation.

The prototype should be strong enough to capture attention from a business team reviewing many submissions.

---

## 2. Core Objective

Create a modern, high-converting, premium-feeling website with:
- Stronger branding and trust
- Better user flow
- Improved call-to-actions
- Increased monetisation opportunities
- Backend ownership (user data, funnels, automation)

---

## 3. Target Audience

- Aspiring property investors
- Beginners looking for education
- People interested in financial freedom
- Users already familiar with Samuel Leeds via social media

---

## 3.1 Technical Stack (Prototype)

- Frontend: Modern responsive web app (mobile-first)
- Backend: Supabase (database, auth, storage)
- Payments: Stripe
- Emails: Resend (transactional + automation)
- Repo: GitHub (version control and deployment workflows)
- Domain: Namecheap (domain purchase and DNS)

Key principle: Even as a prototype, the system should feel real and extensible, not just a static mockup.

## 5. Current Website Issues (IMPROVE / REMOVE)

### 5.1 Design Inconsistency
- Mixed styles across sections
- Lack of unified premium feel
- Feels slightly outdated in places

### 5.2 Visual Hierarchy Problems
- Too many competing elements
- Important actions not clearly prioritised

### 5.3 Weak CTA Strategy
- CTAs not optimised for conversion
- Lack of urgency or clarity
- No structured funnel progression

### 5.4 User Flow Issues
- Journey is not clearly guided
- Users may feel overwhelmed
- No clear "next step" at all times

### 5.5 Over-Reliance on Third-Party Tools
- Webinar platforms
- External systems
- Reduced control over user data

### 5.6 UX Friction
- Elements appearing in odd places (e.g. WhatsApp button)
- Potential distractions
- Inconsistent spacing and layout

### 5.7 Lack of Premium Feel
- Does not fully match revenue scale or brand authority
- Could feel more high-end and intentional

## 9. Psychological Goals

The site should make users feel:
- Trust
- Clarity
- Direction
- Urgency to act
- Confidence in the brand

---

## 10. Success Criteria

The prototype is successful if:
- It looks noticeably more premium
- It improves clarity and flow
- It clearly increases conversion potential
- It demonstrates backend system thinking
- It stands out immediately to reviewers

---

## 11. Positioning

This is not just a redesign.

This is:
- A system upgrade
- A monetisation engine
- A platform shift from marketing site to owned ecosystem

---

## 12. Final Note

The goal is not perfection.

The goal is to create something visually and structurally strong enough that a business team stops and thinks:

"This is actually better than what we currently have."
