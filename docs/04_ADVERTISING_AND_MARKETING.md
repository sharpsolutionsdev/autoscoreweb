## Target Audience

DartVoice targets a specific, passionate, and underserved niche:

### Primary Audience: Home Practice Players
- **Age range:** 25–55
- **Geography:** UK (primary), Europe, North America, Australia/NZ
- **Profile:** Owns a dartboard at home (garage, spare room, man cave). Plays multiple practice sessions per week. Already uses a digital scoring app like Target Dart Counter, Nakka, or DartConnect. Watches PDC events. Engages with darts content on social media.
- **Pain point:** Frustrated by the repetitive interruption of manual score entry. Knows about expensive camera-based auto-scoring (Target Omni, Scolia) but can't justify the £300–500+ cost or physical installation.
- **Spending profile:** Comfortable with subscription software (Netflix, Spotify, etc.). £5–7/month is a no-brainer for something they'd use 3–7 times per week.

### Secondary Audience: League & Pub Players
- Plays in local dart leagues (BDO, WDF affiliates, pub leagues).
- Practices between matches and wants to track consistency.
- Often plays at multiple locations — portability is critical.

### Tertiary Audience: Content Creators & Streamers
- Darts YouTubers and TikTokers who film practice sessions or challenge videos.
- DartVoice's PiP mode and match recording make clean, professional-looking content without visible manual scoring.

## Marketing Channels — Ranked by Priority

### 1. 📱 Short-Form Social Video (TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts)

**Why this is #1:** The DartVoice product demo is *inherently visual and shareable*. A 15-second clip of someone throwing darts, speaking a score, and watching it appear automatically on screen is compelling, self-explanatory content. It generates reactions ("Wait, what?"), saves, and shares — exactly what these algorithms reward.

**Content strategy:**
- **The Demo Hook:** 3-second hook → player throws → says score → DartVoice processes → score appears. Clean, fast, satisfying.
- **The Comparison:** Split-screen. Left side: manual scoring (walk to phone, tap, tap, tap, walk back). Right side: DartVoice (throw, speak, throw). Caption: "Why are you still tapping?"
- **The 180 Moment:** Film a 180 with DartVoice live. The neon "MAXIMUM 180" display is screenshot-worthy.
- **The Setup Tour:** "My £50/year auto-scoring setup vs a £500 camera ring" — resonates with price-conscious players.
- **The Checkout Clutch:** Film a player following DartVoice's checkout suggestion to close a leg. Dramatic, relatable content.

**Posting cadence:** 4–5 pieces of content per week across TikTok + Reels. Repurpose every piece across both platforms.

**Key hashtags:** #darts #dartspractice #501 #darttok #dartsetup #autoscoring #dartvoice #targetdartcounter #pdc

### 4. 👥 Community Engagement (Reddit, Facebook, Forums)

**Why:** Darts communities are tight-knit and highly opinionated. A genuine presence builds trust far more effectively than paid ads.

**Key communities:**
- **Reddit:** r/darts (200K+ members), r/dartscounter
- **Facebook Groups:** "Darts Practice", "Home Dart Setups", "Target Dart Counter Users", regional dart league groups
- **Forums:** Darts Nutz, A180, Winmau forums
- **Discord:** Growing darts Discord servers

**Engagement rules (critical):**
1. **Never spam.** Contribute valuable content first — tips, commentary, answering questions.
2. Product mentions should be organic: "I've been using DartVoice for my sessions, happy to share how it works if anyone's curious."
3. Run occasional AMAs: "I built a voice-controlled dart scorer — AMA."
4. Share user-generated clips from DartVoice sessions in relevant threads.
5. Respond to every mention of auto-scoring or voice scoring with a helpful, non-pushy comment.

### 6. 🏆 Ambassador & Referral Program

**Why:** Word-of-mouth is the most powerful channel in niche communities. Darts players trust recommendations from other darts players.

**Program structure:**
- Every subscriber gets a unique referral link from their dashboard.
-- Referred friend receives a **7-day free trial** (same as the standard trial) — consistent onboarding.
- When the referral converts to a paying subscriber, the ambassador receives **£5 cash via PayPal**. No cap.
- Ambassador dashboard provides real-time tracking: clicks, sign-ups, conversions, pending payouts.
- Minimum payout threshold: £5 (one referral).

**Amplification tactics:**
- Encourage ambassadors to share in darts WhatsApp groups, Facebook groups, and league chats.
- Provide pre-made shareable assets: images, short clips, copy templates.
- Feature top ambassadors on social media (with permission): "This month's top referrer: @DartsDave with 23 referrals."

## Messaging Pillars — For All Marketing

Every piece of content, ad, social post, or email should ladder up to one of these core messages:

1. **"Always in your pocket."** — No hardware. Just your phone.
2. **"Just say your score."** — Zero learning curve. Natural darts language.
3. **"Works with your scorer."** — Not a replacement. A companion.
4. **"From £5/month."** — 100x cheaper than camera systems.
5. **"Try it free."** — 7-day trial, cancel anytime, zero risk.

## What Not To Do

- ❌ **Don't trash competitors publicly.** Respect Target Omni and Scolia. Position DartVoice as the affordable, portable *alternative* — not the "better" product.
- ❌ **Don't over-promise accuracy.** Voice recognition is very good but not perfect. Setting expectations correctly builds trust.
- ❌ **Don't spam darts communities.** Contribute first, mention product second. Getting banned from r/darts would be catastrophic.
- ❌ **Don't use stock photography.** All visuals should be real product screenshots, real setups, or our premium CSS-rendered mockups.
- ❌ **Don't ignore support requests.** In a niche community, one bad support experience travels fast. Respond to every message within 24 hours.
