# Future Vision: Ranked Darts & The MMR System

As DartVoice secures its foundation as a premium utility software, the future roadmap shifts entirely towards revolutionizing competitive home darts through a proprietary **MMR (Matchmaking Rating) System**. 

## 1. Ditching the Traditional "Prize Money Tournaments"
Historically, online darts has relied on ad-hoc prize money tournaments which suffer from rampant cheating, complex administration, and low global engagement. 
DartVoice aims to ditch this archaic model in favor of an always-on, hyper-competitive **Ranked Darts** ladder.

## 2. The MMR (Matchmaking Rating) System
Rather than playing for $5 pots on weekends, players will log in daily to grind their MMR—exactly like competitive video games (*Valorant*, *League of Legends*, *Rocket League*).
- **The Elo Grind:** Every match played through the DartVoice tether affects a player's hidden Elo. 
- **Rank Tiers:** Players are placed into distinct skill buckets (e.g., Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, Diamond, Apex). 
- **Matchmaking:** The system automatically pairs players with exact similar averages over a video call connection, ensuring incredibly tight, thrilling matches every single time. 

## 3. Global Leaderboards & Leagues
- **Open Global Ranking:** A transparent, worldwide leaderboard where the top 500 players fight for the prestigious #1 spot. 
- **Seasonal Leagues:** Instead of constant 1-day tournaments, DartVoice will host structured Seasons (e.g., "Season 1: Summer Climb"). 
- **High-Stakes Queue (Money Tournaments):** While the main queue is purely for MMR and prestige, we may introduce a gated "High Roller" queue where players stake in-app currency or entry fees for heavily moderated, anti-cheat enforced championship brackets.

## 4. The Anti-Cheat Ecosystem
Because DartVoice acts as the input layer, building trust in the Ranked ecosystem is paramount. Future updates will leverage the local software clients to verify audio logic against abnormal throwing patterns to act as an automated referee, preventing "keyboard typing" scores.
